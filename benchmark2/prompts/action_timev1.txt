Classify the risk state of these 3 chronological CCTV frames.

Task:
Detect climb-over-fence or restricted-boundary intrusion.

Risk states:
* normal: no intrusion-related behavior.
* unsafe: near, approaching, checking, touching, waiting, or preparing around the fence/boundary, but not climbing.
* danger: clear climbing or crossing evidence.

Rules:
1. Choose danger when a foot is on a step/fence, the person is already on a step/fence, the person is climbing, gripping the fence for body support, or the body starts crossing the boundary.
2. Even if there is little motion across the frames, choose danger if the person is already on the fence/step or in a climbing posture.
3. Choose unsafe for fence-related approach, checking, touching, waiting, or preparation without clear climbing.
4. Choose normal when the fence is only background and the person shows no fence-directed behavior.

Frame context:
F1: frame={frame_1}, time={time_1}s, action={action_1}
F2: frame={frame_2}, time={time_2}s, action={action_2}
F3: frame={frame_3}, time={time_3}s, action={action_3}

Use images first.
Use actions only as temporal cues.
stand, walk, sit, or none are neutral.
carry/hold, bend/bow, or run/jog can support unsafe near the boundary, but not danger by themselves.

Judge the 3 frames as one sequence.

Return only one JSON object:
{"risk_state":"<normal|unsafe|danger>"}
